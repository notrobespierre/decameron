For my Decameron project, I decided to try to look at how the stories of the Decameron related to each other, and to investigate this I used a variety of computational tools, focusing on two main aspects: Sentiment Analysis and Graphing.

Things that failed and things to revisit

Meaningful data analysis in a short time frame is a bit difficult. One of the first analyses I ran was a Latent Dirichlet Analysis (LDA), with the intent of bringing out common topics and maybe relating them to the people who were speaking.

One limitation of my graph is that it is binarized (there either is or is not a connection), and that many of these themes were selected manually. Right now, every story marked with a theme is connected to every other story marked with that theme, which looks nicely cluttered for the amount of stories I selected, but would look unruly for all 100 stories. In the future, finding specific things that connect stories to reduce the number of connections per node would be a goal.

In the future for the graph, I was thinking of measuring the commonality of a set of words, such as [“woman”, “girl”, “lady”] and measuring the frequency of those words within stories to attempt to find themes that way. I did attempt this, but I was getting issues with the WordNet package, which I was using to get similar words. It would probably be a challenge to get more specific themes, but could provide an interesting measure of how much certain topics are discussed, and give a measure of how prevalent a theme could be in a certain text, and this could possibly add line weights to the graph rather than making them binarized in the way they are now.

Another analysis I think would be interesting is measuring similarity between stories using NLP to see if any other interesting connections arise and if storytellers had similar stories.
